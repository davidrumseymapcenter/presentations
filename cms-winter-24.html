David Rumsey Map Center

The DRMC works to curate and maintain the historical map collections at Stanford Libraries. We serve as a multidisciplinary hub dedicated to advancing education, fostering learning, and supporting research through the use of historical spatial and cartographic data.

For those of you who are not familiar, the David Rumsey Map Center is a unit within Stanford Libraries, which stewards the library's historical cartographic collections. We have a VERy close partnership with David Rumsey

I've been at the Map Center since October 2023, so I'm pretty new in my role. And I want to make this an opportunity to introduce myself a little but more. I've been working with map collections for a while - I've listed my professional places of employment here, but I actually started volunteering in a map library all the way back in 2003 when I was an undergrad student in geography at Western Washington University. Since then, I've just been so curious about the world of map collections and have tried to stay involved in projects and communities I think are at the forefront of research and technology as it relates to sharing and using cartographic collections. So, when I was offered the job at Stanford in October, I was thrilled. Stanford Libraries, and David Rumsey have both been so influential in shaping the way we find and use historical maps over the last few decades, and I'm just so happy to be part of the team.

Beyond the Pillars of Hurcules: A Short History of Maps in Video Games and Virtual Worlds

Tuesday April 2, 2024 | 2:00pm-4:00pm

Short history of video game maps

This talk explores the evolution of maps in video games as a continuation of humanity's fascination with the unknown, symbolized historically by the Pillars of Hercules. Andrea Gatopoulos, a renowned film director, will share insights from his research for the film "A Stranger Quest," delving into how video games reflect and respond to the mysteries of life through their virtual landscapes. Gatopoulos' work, celebrated at major film festivals worldwide, examines virtual realities and the human condition, blending critical perspectives with a yearning for simplicity.

Paper Worlds: Urbano Monte and the Global Rennaissance

May 17-18, 2024

Workshop sponsored by Stanford Department of History and DRMC

This workshop examines Urbano Monte's 1587 world map, held at the Rumsey Map Center, as a significant artifact reflecting the global curiosity and cartographic knowledge of the Renaissance period. We're hosting several scholars from all around the world to speak over that Friday and Saturday. The discussions will aim to contextualize Monte's work within the broader scope of Renaissance cartography and global exploration.
