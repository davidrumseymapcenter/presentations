Hi everyone, I'm Evan Thornberry, Head and Curator of the David Rumsey Map. And - this spring we're turning 10! How many of you here in the room were here at the library whern the map center opened? So for some of you, this might be news. For the rest of you, you might be thinking - wow time really flies. Regardless, 10 years, is something to celebrate. Some days I feel like we're still the new kids on the block, but other days I think about all the accomplishments and partnerships that have been built, and it really hits me how much has happened in a decade.

A Decade in the Making

(But we're just getting started!)

The David Rumsey Map Center opened in 2016 as a partnership between Stanford University Libraries and map collector and general map enthusiast, technical pioneer, and champion for open access: David Rumsey — built on a shared belief that maps are primary sources, teaching tools, and windows into history. The vision from the start: a place where cartographic collections, technology, scholarship, and community could come together to build a real intellectual hub – and ultimately support research and learning across campus and beyond.

A few highlights...


| 2016 Grand opening of the David Rumsey Map Center
| 2017 First Barry Ruderman Conference on Cartography
| 2017 First student essay competition in collaboration with the California Map Society
| 2017 Acquisition of Urbano Monte's 1587 world map
| 2023 Hosted Machines Reading Maps Summit to chart the future of historical cartography as data
| 2024 Paper Worlds: Urbano Monte and the Global Renaissance workshop at the Rumsey Map Center with Paula Findlen
| 2024 AR globe collaboration with Monash University's Embodied Visualisation Lab
| 2025 Colocate² unconference exploring long-term sustainability options for IIIF-enabled tools for maps
Here's a quick highlight reel. A lot has happened in ten years — from the first student exhibitions to major collection acquisitions to cutting-edge AI and AR research partnerships. None of this happened alone.
